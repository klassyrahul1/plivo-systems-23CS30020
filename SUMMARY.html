System Architecture Summary

Submitted by: Reshwik

1. Overview and Constraints

The objective was to achieve an ultra-low latency media stream over a hostile UDP relay, with a target playout delay below 100ms, deadline misses ≤ 1.00%, and bandwidth overhead ≤ 2.00x.

2. Sender Design: Optimized Stride-2 FEC

To defeat burst packet loss, the sender employs a Stride-2 XOR FEC scheme. For each sequence, the sender transmits the media frame and a recovery packet containing the bitwise XOR of the two preceding frames (seq-1 ^ seq-2). FEC packets are thinned to maintain a lean bandwidth overhead of 2.00x.

3. Receiver Design: Sliding Window & Chain-Reaction Recovery

The receiver utilizes a sliding window jitter buffer to hold frames out-of-order. A chain-reaction reconstruction logic runs on every incoming packet, attempting both forward and backward XOR recovery to resolve missing frames immediately. The system achieved a validated 82ms playout delay, flushing packets 2ms early to mitigate system call jitter.
